记录平时遇到的词汇

total some words

unprecedented

  • a. 空前的；无前例的
  • having no precedent; novel
  • exp. …the invention of radio and television, the birth and unprecedented growth of the computer industry, …
  • from ComputerN Networks - Andrew & David Chap1 P1

finalize

  • v. 使结束，使完结，最终确定
  • to complete the last part of a plan, trip, project, etc.
  • exp. Do not finalize the design or standard until multiple prototypes have successfully communicated with each other.

draw on

  • phr. 利用；吸收；戴上；临近
  • exp. This RFC draws heavily on ideas put forth by Clark (1998) and Saltzer et al. (1984).
  • put forth
    • phr. 提出；发表；启航；(植物等) 长出；放出

bank on

  • phr. 指望；依靠；
  • exp. so companies must bank on the fact that the technologies they’re creating today will be what customers are looking for a few years down the road, and that they will beat out products from rivals.

topology

  • n. 地质学；拓扑；拓扑学；布局
  • exp. To achieve its goals, the network layer must know about the topology of the network (i.e., the set of all routers and links) and choose appropriate paths through it, even for large networks.
  • related. topographic
    • a. 地质的；地形学上的
  • related. topography
    • n. 地形学；地形，地貌；局部解剖学；地形图，地势图

freedom of expression

  • phr. 言论自由

intrusion

  • n. 侵入，闯入
  • entrance by force or without permission or welcome
  • exp. The celebrity couple is annoyed by the press intrusion into their affairs.
  • trans. intrude
    • vi. 闯入，侵入
    • vt. 强挤入，把 (自己的思想) 强加于人
    • to become involved with something private in an annoying way
    • exp. The robber intruded into my house with a knife.

aptitude

  • n. 天资；自然倾向；适宜
  • natural ability or skill at doing something
  • exp. He has an excellent aptitude for science.

spectate

  • vt. 出席观看
  • be a spectator in a sports event

industrious

  • a. 勤劳的，勤奋的
  • working hard; busy
  • exp. We should be industrious and hard-working like bees.

whiny

  • a. 烦躁的；爱抱怨的；常发牢骚的
  • habitually complaining

condescending

  • a. 贬低的；有优越感的
  • showing that you believe you are more intelligent or better than other people
  • exp. He has a condescending attitude towards his colleagues.

deprivation

  • n. 剥夺；损失；丧失；免职；匮乏；贫困；
  • the fact of not having something that you need, like enough food, money or a home; the process that causes this
  • exp. The criminal suffered the deprivation of political rights.

hoard

  • v. 贮藏，秘藏
  • to collect and hide a large amount of (something valuable)
  • n. 贮藏物

consensus

  • n. 一致同意，合意
  • an opinion that all members of a group agree with

wail

  • n. 恸哭；哀诉
  • vi. 恸哭；悲叹
  • vt. 哀悼
  • to make a loud, long cry of sadness or pain
  • exp. The baby began to wail when he woke up and his mom wasn’t here.

whimper

  • n. 呜咽；啜泣；怨声
  • a complaint uttered in a plaintive whining way
  • v. 呜咽；啜泣；抽噎地哭；抱怨
  • cry weakly or softly

fiery

  • a. 炽烈的；暴躁的；燃烧般的
  • exp. The fighting, fiery figures are covered in flames.

swagger

  • n. 傲慢的态度，大摇大摆
  • My clothes are cheap, but I wear them with a certain swagger.
    我的衣服很便宜，但是我穿出了逼格。
  • v. 大摇大摆地走，吹牛，傲慢
  • a. 豪华时髦的

contract

  • n. 合同，契约
  • v. 感染（疾病）；招致；订合同；订立（婚约）；缩减
  • to get an illness
  • exp. In California, at least 70 people from one church have contracted the virus.

legislator

  • n. 立法者
  • someone who makes or enacts laws
  • enact
    • v. 颁布；制定法律；扮演
    • to make (a bill or other legislation) officially become part of the law

Christian

  • n. 基督教徒
  • a. 基督教的，基督教徒的

evangelical

  • a. 福音的，热切的
  • n. 福音派信徒
  • exp. Christian evangelicals usually interpret the Bible very literally.

congregation

  • n. 集合在一起的群众
  • exp. The congregation of tourists are following the tour guide into the scenic area.

convalescent

  • a. 恢复期的；渐愈的；调养的
  • of or for or relating to convalescence or convalescents
  • returning to health after illness or debility
  • debility
    • n. 虚弱；衰弱
  • n. 恢复中的病人；康复的人
  • a person who is recovering from illness

at length

  • adv. 最后，终于；详细地

ramp up

  • v. 巩固
  • phr. 提高，斜升，提升
  • bolster or strengthen
  • exp. He is trying to ramp up sales.
    他在努力提升销售额。

inexorable

  • a. 不为所动的，坚决不变的
  • n. 坚定不移的人
  • not able to be stopped or changed
  • exp. My inexorable teacher wouldn’t accept my excuse for the missing homework.

the odds were good

  • phr. 可能性很大
  • The odds were good that I would get the same job after graduate from college.

invoke

  • v. 调用，祈求，恳求，引起
  • to ask for help or protection from (something or someone)

social security

  • phr. 社会保障

at an alarming rate

  • phr. 以惊人的速度

fervent

  • a. 热心的；强烈的；炽热的；热烈的
  • having or showing very strong and sincere feelings about sth

endorse

  • vt. 背书；认可；签署；赞同；在背面签名
  • to say publicly that you support a person, statement or course of action

come with

  • phr. 伴随。。。发生； 从。。。开始；与。。。一起供给
