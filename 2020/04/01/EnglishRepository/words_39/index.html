百词斩 雅思 2020/04/01

total 23 words 1 similar

atlas

  • n. 地图集
  • a book of maps
  • exp. The atlas contains information about all the countries in the world.

bureaucracy

  • n. 官僚主义；政府机构
  • the system of official rules and ways of doing things that a government or an organization has, especially when these seem to be too complicated
  • exp. The bureaucracy always makes people go through unnecessary procedures.

inaugurate

  • v. 为···揭幕；为···举行就职典礼；开展
  • to officially open a building or start an organization with a special ceremony
  • exp. By cutting this ribbon, we inaugurate the opening of the new store!

vent

  • n. 通风口，排气口；
  • exp. The vent takes old air out and pushes fresh air in.
  • v. 发泄 (情绪)，开孔，泄露

dispute

  • n. 争论，争端；
  • an argument or a disagreement between two people, groups or countries; discussion about a subject where there is disagreement
  • exp. They had a dispute over who should be his dance partner.
  • v. 争执，驳斥

resume

  • n. 简历；
  • a written record of your education and the jobs you have done, that you send when you are applying for a job
  • exp. The HR team are examining the resumes of the interviewees.
  • v. 重新开始；继续；
  • vt. 回到；恢复；重新开始

solitary

  • a. 孤独的，单独的，隐居的
  • done alone; without other people; (of a person or an animal) enjoying being alone; frequently spending time alone;
  • exp. A solitary man is standing in the cold wind alone.

qualitative

  • a. (性) 质的，定性的
  • connected with what something is like or how good it is, rather than with how much of it there is
  • exp. Changes in temperature can lead to qualitative differences.

prosecute

  • v. 起诉；做检察官；从事
  • to officially charge somebody with a crime in court
  • exp. He was prosecuted because of owing his friends’ money.

staple

  • v. 用订书钉订；
  • to attach one thing to another using a staple or staples
  • exp. Please staples the two sheets of paper together.
  • n. 订书钉；主食；主要产品；
  • a. 主要的；基本的；标准的

beam

  • n. 光线；(房屋等) 大梁；(横) 梁；
  • a line of light, electric waves or particles
  • exp. A sun beam reaches down through the clouds.
  • v. 发光；照射；发射，播送

commute

  • v. 经常乘车往返于两地之间；
  • to travel regularly by bus, train, car, etc. between your place of work and your home
  • exp. I commute from my home to the office by bus every day.
  • n. 在两地之间乘车往返；
  • vt. 减免

versatile

  • a. 万能的，综合的，多才多艺的
  • (of a person) able to do many different thing; (of food, a building, etc.) having many different uses
  • exp. He’s a versatile musician who can play a wide variety of musical instruments.

verbal

  • a. 言语的，口头的，动词的
  • relating to words; spoken, not written; relating to verbs
  • exp. Thanks to her great verbal skills she delivered a brilliant presentation.

embody

  • v. 体现，象征
  • to express or represent an idea or a quality
  • exp. The lotus flower embodies purity and integrity.

batch

  • n. 一批 (人或物)；一批生产量
  • a number of people or things that are dealt with as a group
  • exp. I just finished this batch of cookies - would you like one?

premise

  • n. 前提；房屋地基；
  • a statement or an idea that forms the basis for a reasonable line of argument
  • exp. This is the movie’s premise; Two people are trapped on an island and they end up falling in love!
  • v. 假定

deviate

  • v. 偏离；背离
  • to be different from something; to do something in a different way from what is usual or expected
  • exp. The hiker has deviated from the main route; let’s hope he can reach his destination.

glacier

  • n. 冰川，冰河
  • a large mass of ice, formed by snow on mountains,
  • exp. The glacier is made up of big blocks of ice.

oversee

  • v. 监督；偷看到，无意中看到
  • to watch somebody / something and make sure that a job or an activity is done correctly
  • exp. The company brought in a new manager to oversee operations.
  • similar. overhear
    • v. 无意中听到，偷听到

prescribe

  • v. 给···开药；命令，指示
  • (of a doctor) to tell somebody to take a particular medicine or have a particular treatment; to write a prescription for a particular medicine, etc.
  • exp. The doctor prescribed me some medicine for my cold.

psychiatry

  • n. 精神病治疗法
  • the study and treatment of mental illness
  • exp. Psychiatry can be very helpful for patients suffering from mental illness.

hitchhike

  • v. 免费搭乘他人便车
  • to get a ride in a passing vehicle by holding out your arm with your thumb up as you stand on the side of the road
  • exp. The girl sitting on the roadside is trying to hitchhike.
