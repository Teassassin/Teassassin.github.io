fracture

  • the fact of something breaking, especially a bone

dilapidated

  • a. 破旧的，破烂的

flourish

  • vi. 繁荣，茂盛，兴旺
  • to develop quickly and be successful or common

pier

  • a long structure built in the sea and joined to the land at one end, often with places of entertainment on it; a long low structure built in a lake, river or the sea and joined to the land at one end, used by boats to allow passengers to get on and off

wage

  • n. 薪水，报酬；
  • v. 开展，进行 (战争、运动)
  • to begin and continue a war, a battle, etc.

cling

  • v. 紧紧抓住；黏着，挨近；依恋，依附；坚持，墨守，忠实于
  • to hold on tightly to somebody / something

  • to gradually become or make something become weaker until it disappears

  • an act of throwing the ball; the way in which it is thrown

    • v. 迫降海上
    • if a pilot ditches an aircraft, or if it ditches, it lands in the sea in an emergency

    • to get rid of something / somebody because you no longer want or need it / them
    • to stay away from school without permission

retrieve

  • v. 取回，重新得到，挽回，检索
  • to bring or get something back, especially from a place where it should not be; to find and get back data or information that has been stored in the memory of a computer; to make a bad situation better; to get back something that was lost

fluctuate

exotic
