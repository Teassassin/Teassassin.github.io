百词斩 雅思 2020/02/27

total 15 words

aggravate

  • v. 使气恼，激怒
  • to annoy somebody, especially deliberately

sabotage

  • v. 怠工，从事破坏活动，故意阻挠；
  • exp. Her ex-boyfriend sabotaged her wedding by destroying the wedding cake.
  • n. 怠工，蓄意破坏，妨碍
  • the act of doing deliberate damage to equipment, transport, machines, etc. to prevent an enemy from using them, or to protest about something; the act of deliberately spoiling something in order to prevent it from being successful

deteriorating

  • v. 恶化；变质；衰退
  • to become worse
  • exp. The air quality of the city is deteriorating.

menace

  • n. 具有危险性的人 (或物)；
  • a person or thing that causes, or may cause serious damage, harm or danger
  • exp. A careless driver on the road is a menace to the public.
  • v. 威胁，威吓

wither

  • v. 枯萎；凋谢；
  • if a plant withers or something withers it, it dries up and dies
  • vt. 使人感觉羞惭或迷惑

indulge

  • v. 沉迷于；放纵；满足
  • to allow yourself to have or do something that you like, especially something that is considered bad for you
  • exp. The woman indulges in her guilty pleasure by eating too many sweet snacks.

autonomy

  • /ɔ:’tɑːnəmɪ/
  • n. 人身自由
  • the freedom for a country, a region or an organization to govern itself independently
  • exp. Parents give the child autonomy to make her favorite juice.

prescibe

  • v. 给···开药；命令，指示
  • (of a doctor) to tell somebody to take a particular medicine or have a particular treatment; to write a prescription for a particular medicine, etc.
  • exp. The doctor prescibed me some medicine for my cold.

scrutiny

  • n. 监视，细看，详细检查，仔细观察
  • the act of carefully examing something especially in a critical way
  • exp. The exam was going on under the scrutiny of the teacher.

cork

  • n. 软木；
  • a light, soft material that is the thick bark of a type of Mediterranean oak tree; a small round object made of cork or plastic, that is used for closing bottles, especially wine bottles
  • v. 塞住，抑制

sovereign

  • a. 至高无上的，具有独立主权的，有最高统治权的；
  • (of a country or state) free to govern itself; completely independent; having complete power or the greatest power in the country
  • n. 最高统治者，君主，元首

pant

  • v. 喘气；喘息；气喘吁吁地说
  • to breathe quickly with short breaths, usually with your mouth open, because you have been doing some physical exercise, or because it is very hot
  • exp. Dogs pant with their mouths when they are tired.

incipient

  • v. 初期的；刚出现的
  • beginning to develop or exist
  • exp. Buds are the incipient stage of flowers.

antecedent

  • n. 祖先，前情；
  • the people in a family who lived in past times
  • adj. 先行的，先驱的

pethos

  • n. 悲怆，哀婉
  • a quality that causes people to feel sympathy and sadness
  • exp. Usually, some movies have pathos, which pull at people’s emotions.
