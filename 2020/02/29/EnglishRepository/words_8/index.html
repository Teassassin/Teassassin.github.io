百词斩 雅思 2020/02/29

total 29 words

fastidious

  • a. 难讨好的，爱挑剔的
  • being careful that every detail of something is correct
  • exp. Cats are very fastidious cleaners.

morbid

  • a. (精神等) 变态的
  • relating to unpleasant subjects (such as death)
  • exp. Morbid obesity appears to be a standout risk for heart failure.

ostensible

  • a. 表面上的，伪装的
  • seeming or said to be true or real but very possibly not true or real
  • exp. The ostensible reason for the wolf’s arrival is to wish the chick a happy birthday.

come to terms (with sb.)

  • phr. 与···达成协议
  • to reach an agreement
  • exp. We came to terms with each other and decided to work together.

fend

  • v. (靠自己) 照顾，照料
  • to do things without help; to do basic activities by yourself
  • exp. The baby can fend for himself well.

overwrought

  • a. 过分紧张的，过度兴奋的
  • very excited or upset
  • exp. She gets overwrought every time she takes the elevator.

languid

  • a. 没精打采的，倦怠的
  • showing or having very little strength, energy, or activity
  • exp. I didn’t sleep last night, so I’ve been languid all day.

toil

  • v. 苦干，跋涉，费力地做；
  • to work very hard for a long time
  • exp. Workers toiled at the construction site.
  • n. 辛苦，苦工，罗网

turf

  • n. 草地，草皮；
  • the upper layer of ground that is made up of grass and plant roots
  • exp. The green turf is nice to walk on.
  • v. 覆草皮

be on edge

  • phr. 紧张不安
  • be nervous
  • exp. He was on edge during the result checking procedure.

manacle

  • v. 上手铐，束缚；
  • n. 手铐
  • either one of a set of two metal rings designed to lock around a person’s wrists or ankles
  • exp. The police locked the manacles around the man’s wrists.

vigilant

  • a. 警戒的，警惕的
  • carefully noticing problems or signs of danger
  • exp. a vigilant cat hid behind the basket, observing her owner.

archives

  • n. 档案室，档案
  • collection of records especially about an institution
  • exp. The archives contain millions of papers.

plummet

  • v. 垂直或突然地堕下，骤然下降
  • to fall straight down
  • exp. The eagle plummets to the earth to catch the rabbit.

precipice

  • n. 悬崖；危险的处境，灾难的边缘，濒危点
  • a very steep side of a mountain or cliff
  • exp. Be careful at the precipice-you might fall off.

well-to-do

  • a. 富有的，有钱的，生活优裕的，小康的
  • having plenty of money and possessions
  • exp. The well-to-do family are happy with their newly built house.

magnate

  • n. 大人物，大亨，巨头，权贵
  • a person who has great wealth and power in a particular business or industry
  • exp. An oil magnate is a very important person.

layoff

  • n. 活动停止期间，临时解雇，操作停止，失业期
  • the act of ending the employment of a worker or group of workers
  • exp. The boss had to make layoffs, so she was fired from her job.

stand in (for sb.)

  • v. 代替
  • be a substitute
  • exp. Since his dad is sick today, he has to stand in for him in the office.

rhetoric

  • n. 修辞学，浮夸的言语
  • language that is used to persuade or influence people, especially language that sounds impressive but is not actually sincere or useful
  • exp. Rhetoric is the study of how to use words to convince people.

amphibian

  • n. 两栖动物。水陆两用飞机和车辆；
  • an animal (such as a frog or toad) that can live both on land and in water
  • exp. Frogs are one kind of amphibian.
  • a. 两栖类的，【车辆】水陆两用的，具有双重性格的

synonym

  • /sɪnənɪm/
  • n. 同义词；同义密码子
  • a word that has the same meaning as another word in the same language
  • exp. There are six pairs of synonyms in the graph.

treatise

  • n. 专著；论述
  • a book, article, etc., that discusses a subject carefully and thoroughly
  • exp. He read a long treatise on medicine.

congestion

  • n. 拥挤，充血
  • excessive crowding
  • exp. This area has really bad traffic congestion.

trespass

  • v. 侵入；
  • to go on someone’s land without permission
  • exp. If you trespass on my land, I can throw you out.
  • n. 非法侵入

pollinate

  • vt. 传花粉给···
  • to give (a plant) pollen from another plant of the same kind so that seeds will be produced
  • exp. Bees pollinate many of the flowers in the garden.

traction

  • n. 拽；牵引；牵引力；附着摩擦力
  • the action of pulling something along a surface; the power that is used for doing this; a way of treating a broken bone in your body that involves using special equipment to pull the bone gradually back into its correct place; the force that stops something, for example the wheels of a vehicle, from sliding on the ground; the extent to which an idea, a product, etc. becomes popular or gains support
  • exp. Wheels move forward by traction.

settle (sb.) in

  • phr. 迁入 (新居)，(使) 习惯
  • settle into a position, usually on a surface or ground
  • exp. Shortly after moving into their new house the family seem to have settled in.

find one’s bearings

  • v. 指明方向
  • find out the right way
  • exp. I could find my bearings using the app on my phone.
