百词斩 雅思 2020/02/26

total 22 words

envisage

  • v. 想象，设想
  • to imagine what will happen in the future

constrict

  • v. 约束，收缩，妨碍
  • to become tighter or narrower; to make something tighter or narrower; to limit or restrict what somebody is able to do
  • exp. This tight corset constricts her body.

这个束腰把她的身体收得很紧

come off

  • phr. 从···掉下，发生，举行，(计划) 实现
  • to fall from something

undermine

  • v. 暗中破坏；逐渐削弱；侵蚀···的基础
  • to make something, especially somebody’s confidence or authority, gradually weaker or less effective
  • to make something weaker at the base, for example by digging under it
  • exp. Cyber-criminals undermine the internet’s security.

ponder

  • v. 仔细考虑，沉思
  • to think about something carefully for a period of time
  • exp. She is pondering over what to wear today.

meditate

  • v. 考虑，沉思，冥想
  • to think deeply, usually in silence, especially for religious reasons or in order to make your mind calm; to plan something in your mind; to consider doing something

overt

  • a. 明显的；公开的；公然的
  • done in an open way and not secretly
  • exp. It is overt that the dog wanted to eat that spaghetti.

advent

  • n. 出现；来临；到来
  • the coming of an important event, person, invention, etc.
  • exp. The advent of stone tools enhanced the hunting abilities of humans.

denounce

  • v. 谴责，斥责
  • to strongly criticize somebody / something that you think is wrong, illegal, etc.

perpetual

  • a. 永远的，永恒的
  • continuing for a long period of time without interruption
  • exp. There is perpetual snowfall on this mountain as it snows all year round.

这座山终年下雪，一直都被冰雪覆盖

dismay

  • n. 沮丧；失望
  • a worried, sad feeling after you have received an unpleasant surprise

endeavour

  • n. 尝试；
  • an attempt to do something, especially something new or difficult
  • v. 努力

elicit

  • v. 探出；诱出；引出
  • to get information or a reaction from somebody, often with difficulty
  • exp. The kid is trying to elicit information from his mother about his birthday gift.

这个小孩试图从妈妈的嘴里探出生日礼物的消息

inherent

  • a. 生来就有的；内在的，固有的
  • that is a basic or permanent part of somebody / something and that cannot be removed

attorney

  • /ə’tɜːrnɪ/
  • n. (辩护) 律师
  • a lawyer, especially one who can act for somebody in court; a person who is given the power to act on behalf of another in business or legal matters
  • exp. The attorney is defending his client in the courtroom.

lubricate

  • v. 使···润滑；给···加润滑油
  • to put a lubricant on something such as the parts of a machine, to help them move smoothly

immense

  • a. 巨大的，广大的
  • extremely large in size or amount

ventilate

  • v. 使···通风，透风
  • to allow fresh air to enter and move around a room, building, etc.
  • exp.She opened the window to ventilate the house.

deplore

  • v. 谴责；对···深感遗憾；悲悼；痛惜
  • to strongly disapprove of something and criticize it, especially publicly

haunt

  • v. (鬼，魂) 常出没于；
  • if the ghost of a dead person haunts a place, people say that they have seen it there
  • exp. Ghosts are believed to haunt old places.
  • n. 常去的地方

tenacious

  • adj. 坚韧不拔的，固执的，顽强的
  • that does not stop holding something or give up something easily; determined; continuing to exist, have influence, etc. for longer than you might expect

manifest

  • adj. 显然的，明白无误的，明了的；
  • (formal) easy to see or understand
  • exp. His nervousness was manifest to all the audience.
  • v. 使显现，使暴露，表明
