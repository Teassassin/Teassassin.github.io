百词斩 雅思 2020/03/26

total 15 words 1 similar

coil

  • n. (一) 卷，(一) 圈；
  • a series of circles formed by winding up a length of rope, wire, etc.
  • exp. This is a coil of metal wire.

riot

  • v. 闹事；
  • (of a crowd of people) to behave in a violent way in a public place, often as a protest
  • exp. The angry villagers are rioting in the street.
  • n. 骚乱；暴乱；骚动；放纵

observance

  • n. 庆祝，礼仪，仪式；(法律习俗等的) 遵守
  • the practice of obeying a law, celebrating a festival or behaving according to particular custom
  • exp. In observance of Christmas, we put up a tree and give each other girls.

oblige

  • v. 迫使，使感激，施恩于，帮忙，使···成为必要
  • to force somebody to do something, by law, because it is a duty, etc.; to help somebody by doing what they ask or what you know they want
  • exp. Tax payers are obliged to pay tax accordingly.

mortgage

  • n. 按揭贷款，抵押贷款；
  • a legal agreement by which a bank or similar organization lends you money to buy a house, etc., and you pay the money back over a particular number of years; the sum of money that you borrow
  • exp. He took out a mortgage, meaning he borrowed money to buy his home.
  • v. 抵押

clear up

  • v. 放晴，清楚，整理

monologue

  • n. 独白，个人长篇演说
  • a long speech by one person during a conversation that stops other people from speaking or expressing an opinion; a long speech in a play, film / movie, etc. spoken by one actor, especially when alone; a dramatic story, especially in verse, told or performed by one person
  • exp. The entire play was just a two hour monologue by one person; it was so boring!

impromptu

  • a. 即席的，即兴的；
  • done without preparation or planning
  • exp. At the command of his brother, he composed an impromptu poem within seven paces.
  • adv. 即席地，即兴地；
  • n. 即兴之作

surmount

  • v. 克服，战胜，超过
  • to deal successfully with a difficulty; to be placed on top of something
  • exp. The man surmounts the house and rides it forward.

aesthetic

  • a. 美学的；审美的；悦目的；雅致的
  • concerned with beauty and art and the understanding of beautiful things
  • exp. The oil painting has great aesthetic value.

bowel

  • n. 肠；内部
  • the tube along which food passes after it has been through the stomach, especially the end where waste is collected before it is passed out of the body
  • exp. The doctor tells me that my bowel is very healthy.

tuck

  • v. 把 (衬衫、餐巾等的) 边塞到里面；
  • to push, fold or turn the ends or edges of clothes, paper, etc. so that they are held in place or look neat
  • exp. I tucked my blouse into my skirt to look presentable.
  • n. (衣服等的) 褶，裥

bring in

  • v. 增加，赚取，产生，引进
  • to attract somebody / something to a place or business; to make or earn a particular amount of money
  • exp. The city brought in lots of talented people.

groan

  • v. 呻吟；抱怨；
  • to make a long deep sound because you are annoyed, upset or in pain, or with pleasure
  • exp. The woman loudly groaned due to her splitting headache.
  • n. 呻吟

overhear

  • v. 无意中听到，偷听到
  • to hear, especially by accident, a conversation in which you are not involved
  • exp. This father overheard his children talking.
  • similar. oversee
    • v. 监督；偷看到，无意中看到
