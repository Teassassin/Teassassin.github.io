百词斩 雅思 2020/03/13

total 17 words

remnant

  • n. 遗迹；边角剩料
  • a part of something that is left after the other parts have been used, removed, destroyed, etc.
  • exp. The remnant of Yuanmingyuan becomes a tourist attraction today.

predominant

  • a. 占主导地位的；显著的
  • having more power or influence than others
  • exp. The white pieces were predominant on the chess board, so I lost the game.

barter

  • v. 物物交换；以货易货
  • to exchange goods, property, services, etc. for other goods, etc. without using money
  • exp. He bartered the fruits for the rabbit.

drudgery

  • n. 苦工，苦差事，单调乏味的工作
  • boring, difficult, or unpleasant work
  • exp. I hate my job - it’s just a bunch of mindless drudgery.

come round (around)

  • phr. 改变主意；重又来到
  • change one’s position or opinion
  • exp. I thought he’d never agree, but in the end he came around.

terraces

  • n. 梯田
  • a flat, hard area, especially outside a house or restaurant, where you can sit, eat and enjoy the sun
  • exp. This area is famous for its beautiful rice terraces.

zoology

  • n. 动物学；动物区系
  • the branch of science that involves that study of animals and animal behavior
  • exp. The boy is reading a book about zoology.

crude

  • n. 原油；
  • oil in its natural state, before it has been treated with chemicals
  • exp. The middle east is rich in crude oil.
  • a. 天然的；粗糙的；粗鲁的；简陋的

stand off

  • phr. 远离；疏远
  • the act of repulsing or repelling an attack; a successful defensive stand
  • exp. They’ve been arguing for hours, and there’s no sign the stand off will end.

counterbalance

  • v. 使平衡；抵消；
  • to have an equal but opposite effect to something else
  • exp. Add some honey to counterbalance the acidity of the lemon.
  • n. 抵销

wardrobe

  • n. 衣橱；全部服装
  • a large cupboard for hanging clothes in which is either a piece of furniture or (in British English) built into the wall
  • exp. All my clothes are in this wardrobe.

opening

  • n. 开口；孔；开始；开幕；职位空缺
  • a space or hole that somebody / something can pass through
  • exp. There’s a small opening near the back; we can exit there.

nuance

  • n. 细微差别
  • a very small difference in color, tone, meaning, etc.
  • exp. There are some nuances in appearance of the twin sisters.

explicit

  • a. 充分表达清楚的，明确的
  • (of a statement or piece of writing) clear and easy to understand
  • exp. The map shows an explicit route to me.

shiver

  • v. 颤抖，打战；颤动；
  • (of a person) to shake slightly because you are cold, frightened, exicited, etc.
  • exp. The man’s hand is shivering violently.
  • n. 颤抖，打战

appraisal

  • n. 评估；估价；评价
  • a judgement of the value, performance or nature of somebody / something
  • exp. His appraisal of the house shows that it isn’t very valuable.

make for sb.(sth.)

  • phr. 有助于
  • cause to happen or to occur as a consequence
  • exp. Fresh vegetable makes for your health.
