百词斩 雅思 2020/03/06

total 20 words

flutter

  • v. 飘动，飘扬；(鸟等) 振翼，排翅而飞；
  • to move lightly and quickly; to make something move in this way
  • exp. The white flag flutters in the wind.
  • n. 激动不安，紧张

single out

  • v. 挑选，选出
  • select from a group
  • exp. He always singles out good apples for sale.

grim

  • a. 严酷的，无情的；令人沮丧的，令人失望的
  • looking or sounding very serious
  • exp. It’s very hard for the patient to accept the grim result.

magnanimous

  • a. 宽宏大量的，大度的
  • having or showing a generous and kind nature
  • exp. He was magnanimous in defeat and praised his opponent’s skill.

mar

  • v. 毁掉，弄糟，玷污
  • to damage or spoil something good
  • exp. The wedding was marred by the terrible weather.

bungalow

  • n. 平房，小屋
  • a house built all on one level, without stairs
  • exp. A bungalow is a house which has only one level, and no stairs.

detest

  • vt. 厌恶，憎恨，痛恨
  • to dislike (someone or something) very strongly
  • exp. The internet bullies bothered the boy because they detest him so much.

take sth. over

  • v. 接管
  • seize and take control without authority and possibly with force; take as one’s right or possession

crooked

  • a. 弯曲的；歪的；不正当的；不正直的
  • not in a straight line; bent or twisted
  • exp. My teeth are so crooked.

trifle

  • n. 蛋糕
  • slightly; something that is not valuable or important; a cold dessert (= a sweet dish) made from cake and fruit with wine and / or jelly poured over it, covered with custard and cream
  • exp. There are two strawberry trifles on the desk.

threshold

  • n. 门槛；开端
  • the floor or ground at the bottom of a doorway, considered as the entrance to a building or room
  • exp. As you step over the threshold, you will change into a new person.

hard and fast

  • phr. (规则等) 严格的，不容改变的
  • (of rules) stringently enforced
  • exp. My country has hard and fast rules and regulations.

versed

  • a. 熟练的，精通的
  • having knowledge about something
  • exp. The student is well versed in all kinds of subjects.

barometer

  • n. 晴雨表；气压表
  • an instrument for measuring air pressure to show when the weather will change
  • exp. The needle on the barometer is indicating sunny weather.

outweigh

  • v. 比···重要；比···有价值
  • to be greater or more important than something
  • exp. There is something that outweighs money, such as love.

abound

  • v. 充满，很多，富于
  • to exist in great numbers or quantities
  • exp. Grapes abound in his farm.

indignant

  • a. 愤慨的，愤愤不平的
  • feeling or showing anger and surprise because you think that you have been treated unfairly
  • exp. He is indignant about the unfair treatment that the dog received.

tacit

  • a. 心照不宣的，静默的
  • expressed or understood without being directly stated
  • exp. They exchanged a tacit glance when passing each other.

fall back on

  • phr. 求助于
  • depend on
  • exp. When I lost my job, I had to fall back on begging for money.

porous

  • a. 可渗透的，多孔的，可穿越的
  • having many small holes that allow water or air to pass through slowly
  • exp. Sponges are porous so they can absorb a lot of water.
