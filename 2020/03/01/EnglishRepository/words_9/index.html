百词斩 雅思 2020/03/01

total 26 words, 2 similar

become of

  • v. (命运等) 降临，使遭遇，发生···情况
  • to happen to
  • exp. “What has become of my car?!”

oblique

  • a. 斜的，非直角的，隐射的；
  • not direct : not stated directly
  • v. 倾斜
  • exp. The red box is set at an oblique angle to the rest of the boxes.

interrogate

  • v. 审问，审讯
  • to ask someone a lot of questions for a long time in order to get information, sometimes using threats
  • exp. The dog interrogated the kitten to find out the truth about his missing dinner.

palpitate

  • v. (心脏) 悸动，发抖
  • to beat quickly and strongly and often in a way that is not regular because of excitement, nervousness, etc.
  • exp. My heart palpitates when I see the beautiful girl.

plagiarize

  • /‘pleɪdʒəraɪz/
  • v. 剽窃，抄袭 (别人学说、著作)
  • to use the words or ideas of another person as if they were your own words or ideas
  • exp. He plagiarized his essay content from other people’s work.

oblong

  • n. 长方形，椭圆形；
  • having four straight sides that meet at right angles and having two opposite sides that are short and two opposite sides that are long
  • exp. My computer screen is oblong in shape.
  • a. 长方形

lose one’s bearings

  • phr. 迷失方向，不知所措
  • get lost; lose one’s way
  • similar. find one’s bearings
    • v. 指明方向

rehabilitate

  • v. 使康复，使恢复名誉，使恢复原状
  • to bring (someone or something) back to a normal, healthy condition after an illness, injury, drug problem, etc.
  • exp. We will help rehabilitate your legs, until you can walk on your own again.

defiled

  • v. 污损，弄脏；以纵队前进；
  • to make (something) dirty
  • exp. The factory defined the river with sewage and waste.
  • n. 隘路

colossal

  • /kə’lɑːsl/
  • a. 巨大的，庞大的；巨像 (似) 的
  • very large or great
  • exp. That’s one colossal pumpkin.

custodian

  • n. 监护人，保管人，看守人
  • a person who cleans and takes care of a building
  • exp. He is happy to be a custodian of this forest.

bear sth. (sb.) out

  • v. 证实
  • confirm, substantiate
  • exp. He had an idea about the criminal’s identity, and looking at the fingerprints bears his idea out.

put an end to

  • v. 停止，消灭
  • kill an masse; kill on a large scale; kill many
  • exp. The new spray puts an end to ants and other bugs.

get at sb. (sth.)

  • v. 到达，得到
  • to reach effectively
  • exp. I used a ladder to get at the apple.

neurosis

  • n. 【心理】神经症，神经衰弱症
  • an emotional illness in which a person experiences strong feelings of fear or worry
  • exp. Lack her sleep made her neurosis come to the fore.

pungent

  • a. 刺鼻的；刺激性的
  • having a strong taste or smell
  • exp. The onions are very pungent! I’ll have to shut my nose.

maim

  • v. 使残疾
  • injure so badly that part of body is permanently damage
  • exp. The soldier was maimed during the war, suffering the loss of a limb.

mundane

  • a. 单调的；普通的，平凡的
  • not interesting or exciting
  • exp. His life is much more mundane compared to that of his friend.

brashness

  • n. 莽撞，无礼，轻率
  • the trait of being rash and hasty
  • exp. His brashness and drunken antics embarrass both himself and the poor waiter.
    他的无礼和醉酒行为不仅把自己陷入难堪的境地，也让可怜的侍者手足无措。
    • antics
    • n. 滑稽动作；古怪姿态

underwrite

  • v. 给···保险，承诺支付；同意赔款 (海上保险)
  • to give money to support (something, such as a new bussiness) and agree to be responsible for any losses if it fails
  • exp. OK, I’ll underwrite the loan if you promise you can pay.

at the mercy of

  • prep. 在···支配中，任凭···摆布
  • under the control of
  • exp. I’m at the mercy of my boss; he always yells at me.

infest

  • v. 骚扰，寄生于，大批出没
  • to be in or over (a place, an animal, etc.) in large numbers
  • exp. The warehouse is infested with rats.

misgiving

  • n. 疑虑
  • a feeling of doubt about something : a feeling that something might not be right or might not work as planned
  • When I told him the deal, he said he had some misgivings–maybe he didn’t want the deal at all.

vogue

  • n. 时髦，时尚；
  • something (such as a way of dressing or behaving) that is fashionable or popular in a particular time and place
  • exp. Red dresses are really in vogue this year.
  • a. 流行的
  • similar. vague
    • a. 含糊的，模糊的，不明确的，茫然的
    • n. 模糊不定状态

perennial

  • a. 四季不断的；终年的，永久的；
  • a plant that lives for several years or for many years : a perennial plant
  • exp. If a plant can last for three seasons or more, it is perennial.

perturb

  • vt. 使不安；使紊乱
  • to cause (someone) to be worried or upset
  • exp. The coming test perturbs the unprepared student.
