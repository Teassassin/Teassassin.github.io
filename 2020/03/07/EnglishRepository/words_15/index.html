百词斩 雅思 2020/03/07

total 23 words

shanty

  • n. 简陋的小木屋；(水手唱的) 劳动号子
  • a small, simple building that is roughly made from sheets of wood, plastic, etc., and that is used as a house by poor people
  • exp. The abandoned shanty in the forest looks terrible.

wretched

  • a. 可怜的
  • (of a person) feeling ill / sick or unhappy; making you feel sympathy or pity
  • exp. The wretched old lady lives in poor conditions.

make (sth.) out

  • v. 辨认出
  • detect with the senses
  • exp. With all the fog, I can just barely make out his face.

wary

  • a. 谨慎的，小心的，提防的
  • careful when dealing with somebody / something because you think that there may be a danger or problem
  • exp. The cat has a wary stare at someone coming close.

exasperate

  • vt. 激怒
  • to make (someone) very angry or annoyed
  • exp. The naughty boy exasperated the man by firing his slingshot at him.

beverage

  • n. (水，酒等之外的) 饮料
  • any type of drink except water
  • exp. We have a selection of different beverages: what would you like to drink?

mesh

  • n. 网，筛孔；
  • exp. We made a fence out of this wire mesh.
  • v. 相啮合

entangle

  • v. 使纠缠，卷入
  • to cause (something) to get caught
  • exp. Damn! My hair has become entangled into a knot.

swerve

  • v. 转弯，突然改变方向
  • to change direction suddenly especially to avoid hitting someone or something
  • exp. The car swerved to avoid hitting the dog.

tamper

  • v. 篡改，玩弄，贿赂；
  • to change or touch (something) especially in a way that causes damage or harm
  • exp. I had to tamper with my mark, otherwise my parents would have kicked my ass.
  • n. 填塞者，捣棒

sanitary

  • a. 卫生的；清洁的
  • clean; not likely to cause health problems
  • exp. You should wash your hands for sanitary reasons.

petal

  • n. 花瓣
  • a delicate coloured part of a flower. The head of a flower is usually made up of several petals around a central part.
  • exp. The colourful flower petals cover the ground.

phonetic

  • a. 语音的
  • representing each speech sound with a single symbol
  • exp. Phonetic symbols help us to pronounce different words properly.

set in

  • phr. 开始，到来，生效，流行，设置 (戏剧、小说等的) 背景，上涨，插入
  • exp. We’ll have to wait until the drugs set in and she falls asleep.

latent

  • a. 潜在的，隐伏的。不易察觉的
  • existing, but not yet very noticeable, active or well developed
  • exp. The latent talent of a child is enormous.

trauma

  • n. 精神创伤，外伤
  • a very difficult or unpleasant experience that causes someone to have mental or emotional problems usually for a long time
  • exp. Soldiers often suffer from psychological trauma.

indigenous

  • a. 土生土长的，本土的
  • produced, living, or existing naturally in a particular region or environment
  • exp. Indians were the first indigenous people of the United States.

get nowhere

  • phr. (使) 无进展，行不通
  • accomplish nothing
  • exp. I came up with many ideas, but I got nowhere with them.

expedient

  • a. 有利的，方便的；权宜之计的；
  • providing an easy and quick way to solve a problem or do something
  • exp. We found it expedient to just go straight through the complicated maze.

norm

  • n. 规范，标准；准则；模范
  • standards of behaviour that are typical of or accepted within a particular group or society
  • exp. The students should follow the norms of conduct for the school.

archaic

  • a. 已不通用的；陈旧的，过时的，老式的
  • old and no longer used
  • exp. Radios are becoming archaic today.

conspicuous

  • a. 显眼的；显著的；显而易见的
  • easy to see or notice; likely to attract attention
  • exp. The red man is very conspicuous, since everyone else is blue.
