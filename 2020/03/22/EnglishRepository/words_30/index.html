百词斩 雅思 2020/03/22

total 20 words

retain

  • v. 保持，保留，保存
  • to keep something; to continue to have something; to continue to hold or contain something;
  • exp. Hot water can retain its heat for hours in the vacuum cup.

decimal

  • n. 小数；
  • a fraction (= a number less than one) that is shown as a dot or point followed by the number of tenths, hundredths, etc.
  • exp. The decimal 0.5 is less than 1.
  • a. 小数的；十进制的

scorn

  • n. 鄙视；
  • a strong feeling that somebody / something is stupid or not good enough, usually shown by the way you speak
  • exp. She scorned her work colleague who just read magazines all day.
  • v. 轻蔑；对···不屑一顾

overwhelm

  • v. 压垮；打败，压倒
  • to have such a strong emotional effect on somebody that it is difficult for them to resist or know how to react
  • exp. She is being totally overwhelmed by her work load.

speculate

  • vt. 推测；
  • to form an opinion about something without knowing all thee details or facts
  • exp. I speculate the trend of foreign exchange market to make money.
  • vi. 投机，做投机买卖；推测，猜测

defiance

  • n. 蔑视；抗议，反抗；挑战
  • the act of defying someone or something; a refusal to obey something or someone
  • exp. There’s a look of defiance on his face.

grant

  • v. 给予，许可，授予；
  • to agree to give somebody what they ask for, especially formal or legal permission to do something
  • exp. The Subway Committee grants the blind permission to bring guide dogs on board.
  • n. 许可

expel

  • v. 罚出，开除；排出；开除；驱逐
  • to officially make somebody leave a school or an organization
  • exp. The referee expels a player from the game for not playing by the rules.

orthodox

  • a. 正统的
  • (especially of beliefs or behavior) generally accepted or approved of; following generally accepted beliefs
  • exp. The Greek orthodox church with blue domes is so beautiful.

spill

  • v. 使流出；浇 (花等) ；
  • to cause or allow (something) to fall, flow, or run over the edge of a container usually in an accidental way
  • exp. Oh no, I’ve spilled the green nail polish.
  • n. 溢出，溅出

superficial

  • a. 表面的；肤浅的；微不足道的；
  • not studying or looking at something thoroughly; seeing only what is obvious
  • exp. The house may look new, but it’s all superficial.
  • n. 表面文章

eminent

  • a. 杰出的
  • (of people) famous and respected, especially in a particular profession
  • exp. He is the eminent Chinese writer who won the Nobel Prize.

graze

  • v. (牲畜) 吃草；
  • (of cows, sheep, etc.) to eat grass that is growing in a field
  • exp. Cows are grazing on the grass in the meadow.
  • n. 擦，擦伤

odd

  • a. 奇怪的；古怪的；奇数的
  • strange or unusual
  • exp. It is so odd of him to be like this.

stipulate

  • v. 规定；保证；要求以···为条件
  • to state clearly and firmly that something must be done, or how it must be done
  • exp. The confidentially agreements stipulate that moviegoers can not reveal any plot of the movie.
    保密协议规定，所有观影人不得透露影片的任何情节。

seam

  • n. 接缝；缝；地层
  • a line along which two edges of cloth, etc. are joined or sewn together
  • exp. There are seams on this white cloth.

avert

  • v. 转移 (目光，注意力等) ；防止；避开
  • to turn your eyes, etc. away from something that you do not want to see
  • exp. They all averted their eyes from each other.

thermal

  • a. 保暖的；温泉的；
  • (of clothing) designed to keep you warm by preventing heat from escaping from the body
  • exp. Gloves, scarfs and thermal underwear help keep you warm in winter.
  • n. 保暖内衣裤

succession

  • n. 一连串；连续，继续
  • a number of people or things that follow each other in time or order
  • exp. A succession of horses passed us in the forest.

exquisite

  • a. 精致的，精选的
  • extremely beautiful or carefully made
  • exp. I’ve spent all my cash to buy you this exquisite necklace.
