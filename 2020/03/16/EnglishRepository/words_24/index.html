百词斩 雅思 2020/03/16

total 18 words

superfluous

  • a. 多余的，过剩的
  • more than you need or want
  • exp. It is superfluous to add feet to the snake while painting.

ore

  • n. 矿，矿石；矿砂
  • rock, earth, etc. from which metal can be obtained
  • exp. These beautiful ores are the material

dilute

  • v. 使变淡，稀释；使病弱
  • to make a liquid weaker by adding water or another liquid to it
  • exp. Water dilutes the solution quickly.

propaganda

  • n. 宣传；宣传方法
  • ideas or statements that may be false or exaggerated and that are used in order to gain support for a political leader, party, etc.
  • exp. The political propaganda is posted onto the lampposts around the city.

contemporary

  • a. 当代的，现代的；同时代的；
  • belonging to the present time
  • exp. As a contemporary writer, his books appealed more to a youthful audience.
  • n. 同龄人，同时代的人

vocational

  • a. 职业的；业务的
  • connected with the skills, knowledge, etc. that you need to have in order to do a particular job
  • exp. I learned how to drive this in vocational school.

clutch

  • v. 紧握；抓住；
  • to hold somebody / something tightly
  • exp. He clutches his red packet and stays alert.
  • n. 抓住；离合器

grope

  • v. 摸索，探索，动手动脚
  • to try and find something that you cannot see, by feeling with your hands; to try and reach a place by feeling with your hands because you cannot see clearly; to touch somebody sexually, especially when they do not want you to
  • exp. “Is anybody there?” he asked as he groped for the light switch.

erosion

  • n. 侵蚀；磨损；削弱；减少
  • the process by which the surface of something is gradually destroyed through the action of wind, rain, etc.
  • exp. The strange shape of the rock was caused by wind erosion.

brittle

  • a. 易碎的；脆弱的；靠不住的
  • hard but easily broken
  • exp. The wine glass looks brittle. Use it carefully!

oust

  • v. 罢免，驱逐，剥夺，取代
  • to force somebody out of a job or position of power, especially in order to take their place
  • exp. The traitor was ousted from the group.

agitation

  • n. 焦虑，忧虑；摇动
  • worry and anxiety that you show by behaving in a nervous way
  • exp. Apparently she is in a state of agitation.

drastic

  • a. 极端的；猛烈的；激烈的
  • extreme in a way that has a sudden, serious or violent effect on something
  • exp. The weather changes are so drastic and unpredictable.

glossary

  • n. 词汇表，术语汇编
  • a list of technical or special words, especially those in a particular text, explaining their meanings
  • exp. You should preview the new words in the glossary before going to class.

aggregate

  • n. 合计；总数；
  • a total number or amount made up of smaller amounts that are collected together
  • exp. The player earned an aggregate of $48000000 from sponsorship deals.
  • a. 总的，总数的；
  • v. 集合

cavity

  • n. 龋洞
  • a hole in a tooth
  • exp. Mr. Tooth is in a great deal of pain due to the cavity on his body.

choir

  • n. (教会的) 歌唱队，唱诗班
  • a group of people who sing together, for example in church services or public performances
  • exp. A penguin choir sings Christmas songs.

yoke

  • n. 牛轭；枷锁；纽带；
  • a long piece of wood that is fastened across the necks of two animals, especially oxen, so that they can pull heavy loads
  • exp. The two oxen are attached together by a yoke over their necks.
