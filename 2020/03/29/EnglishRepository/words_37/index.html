百词斩 雅思 2020/03/29

total 15 words

hedge

  • n. 篱笆，树篱；保障
  • a row of bushes or small trees planted close together, usually along the edge of a field, garden / yard or road
  • exp. The man is trimming the green hedge.

agenda

  • n. 议事日程
  • a list of items to be discussed at a meeting
  • exp. What topics should we put on the agenda for our afternoon meeting?

apt

  • a. 恰当的；有 (做某事) 倾向
  • suitable or appropriate in the circumstances
  • exp. What he said was an apt description of the situation.

instinct

  • n. 天性；本能；直觉
  • a natural tendency for people and animals to behave in a particular way using the knowledge and abilities that they were born with rather than thought or training
  • exp. Even well-fed cats will kill mice, because of their hunting instincts.

disc

  • n. 唱片；[计]磁盘，磁碟片
  • a thin round piece of plastic on which music, etc. is recorded
  • exp. The music is stored in the disc.

brink

  • n. (尤指崖边) 边缘
  • if you are on the brink of something, you are almost in a very new, dangerous or exciting situation; the extreme edge of land, for example at the top of aa cliff or by a river
  • exp. Be careful on the brink - you might fall off.

obscure

  • v. 隐藏，使···模糊；
  • to make it difficult to see, hear or understand something
  • exp. The view from the bridge was obscured by mist.
  • a. 难以理解的；不清楚的

ambiguity

  • n. 模棱两可
  • the state of having more than one possible meaning; a word or statement that can be understood in more than one way
  • exp. Ambiguities arose from the confusing picture.

torrent

  • n. 湍流，洪流，奔流
  • a large amount of water moving very quickly
  • exp. After the storm, torrents of water rushed down the river.

locality

  • n. 地区；位置；方位
  • the place where somebody / something exists; the area that surrounds the place you are in or are talking about
  • exp. This locality is famous for growing pineapples.

preface

  • n. 序言，前言，开端
  • an introduction to a book. especially one that explains the author’s aims
  • exp. The book’s preface is a good introduction to the main text.

denote

  • v. 指出；
  • to mean something
  • exp. The red color is usually used to denote error.
  • vt. 代表 (或表示)

quench

  • v. 止渴；扑灭 (火)
  • to drink so that you no longer feel thirsty
  • exp. The tennis player quenches her thirst with water.

throughout

  • prep. 在···期间，贯穿；遍及；
  • in or into every part of something
  • exp. It snowed throughout the day and night.
  • adv. 到处

degrade

  • v. 贬低；降低品格 (身价、价值等)
  • to show or treat somebody in a way that makes them seem not worth any respect or not worth taking seriously
  • exp. He was degraded from a marshal to a pig.
