百词斩 雅思 2020/03/20

total 37 words

legitimate

  • a. 合法的；合理的；
  • allowed and acceptable according to the law
  • exp. Fishing is legitimate there.
  • vt. 使合法；授权；宣布···为合法

hamper

  • v. 阻碍，妨碍，牵制；
  • to prevent somebody from easily doing or achieving something
  • exp. This huge ball hampered the man’s progress on the obstacle course.
  • n. (有盖的) 大篮子

devise

  • v. 设计，想出；遗赠 (不动产)；
  • to invent something new or a new way of doing something
  • exp. They devised a plan for their company’s future.
  • n. 设计，发明，遗赠

pretext

  • n. 借口，托词
  • a false reason that you give for doing something usually something bad, in order to hide the real reason; an excuse
  • exp. The pupil asked for leave on the pretext of having a stomachache.

stale

  • a. 不新鲜的；走了味的；陈腐的；
  • (of food, especially bread and cake) no longer fresh and therefore unpleasant to eat
  • exp. There is a stale loaf of bread on the plate.
  • v. 变得不新鲜；使陈旧

desolate

  • a. 荒凉的；孤单寂寞的
  • (of a place) empty and without people, making you feel sad or frightened
  • exp. The landscape is desolate: nothing can survive there.

sieve

  • n. 筛子，粗篮；
  • to put something through a sieve
  • exp. The woman is sifting flour through the sieve.
  • v. 筛，滤

snatch

  • v. 强夺；攫取；
  • to take something quickly and often rudely or roughly
  • exp. My brother snatched my Barbie doll from my hands.
  • n. 片断

compatible

  • a. 可和谐共存的
  • able to exist together without trouble or conflict: going together well
  • exp. He is compatible with his friend because of their similar interests and opinions.
  • similar. compact
    • a. 小巧的，小型的，袖珍的，体积小的；紧密的，紧凑的，密集的
    • v. 把···压紧；变得坚实
    • n. 带镜小粉盒；合约，条约

eternal

  • a. 永恒的，永不改变的
  • without an end; existing or continuing forever
  • exp. The lock and key symbolize the eternal love of the couple.

revise

  • v. 修改；温习 (功课)
  • to change your opinions or plans, for example because of something you have learned; to change something, such as a book or an estimate, in order to correct or improve it
  • exp. Please revise your paper - it needs some corrctions and improvements.

deduce

  • v. 推论，腿短；演绎
  • to form an opinion about something based on the information or evidence that is available
  • exp. The detective deduced that the criminal is blonde and without one finger.

subdue

  • v. 征服，压制，减轻
  • to bring somebody / something under control, especially by using force; to calm or control your feelings
  • exp. The police subdue the criminal and pin him to the ground.

chronic

  • a. (疾病) 慢性的；长期的；(人) 久病的；积习难改的；严重的
  • (especially of a disease) lasting for a long time
  • exp. I suffer from chronic pain.

slack

  • a. 松弛的；懈怠的；萧条的；
  • not stretched tight
  • exp. It’s easier to walk across a slack rope than a tight one.
  • v. 变松弛，逃避工作

dread

  • v. 惧怕，害怕；
  • to be very afraid of something; to fear that something bad is going to happen
  • exp. The child dreads the ghost so much that he couldn’t fall asleep.
  • n. 畏惧，恐惧；
  • a. 恐怖的，使人敬畏的

refrain

  • v. 抑制，避免；
  • to stop yourself from doing something, especially something that you want to do
  • exp. Please refrain from talking on your cell phone in the theater.
  • n. 歌曲的反复句，叠句

earnest

  • a. 真诚的，诚挚的，认真的
  • very serious and sincere
  • exp. “Thank you very much!” said the granny with an earnest expression on her face.

clasp

  • v. 扣紧；握紧；扣住；抱紧；
  • to hold something tightly in your hand
  • exp. He clasped his hands together to pray.
  • n. 扣子；钩子；紧抱；紧握

enlighten

  • v. 启发，开导
  • to give somebody information so that they understand something better
  • exp. The teacher really enlightens his students.

feeble

  • a. 虚弱的
  • very weak; not effective; not showing determination or energy
  • exp. The feeble old man found it difficult to climb the stairs.

superb

  • a. 极好的；壮丽的
  • excellent; of very good quality
  • exp. Her piano performance is superb.

batter

  • n. (用鸡蛋、牛奶等调制的) 面糊；
  • a mixture of eggs, milk and flour used in cooking to cover food such as fish or chicken before you fry it, or to make pancakes; a mixture of eggs, milk, flour, etc. used for making cakes
  • exp. The mother is teaching her daughter how to make pancake batter.
  • v. 连续猛击

auxiliary

  • a. 备用的，辅助的，协助的
  • (of workers) giving help or support to the main group of workers; (of a piece of equipment) used if there is a problem with the main piece of equipment
  • exp. We have an auxiliary generator to deal with sudden black-outs.

take-away

  • phr. 外带，外卖
  • a restaurant that cooks and sells food that you take away and eat somewhere else
  • exp. I don’t want to eat the pizza here; I want some take-away.

nominal

  • a. 名义上的；象征性的，很少的；名词性的
  • being something in name only, and not in reality
  • exp. The emperor is just a nominal ruler.

conform

  • vi. 遵照，顺从；
  • to obey a rule, law, etc.
  • exp. Even the donkeys conform to traffic regulations!
  • vt. 使一致；
  • a. 一致的

wield

  • v. 挥；运用，行使
  • to hold something, ready to use it as a weapon or tool
  • exp. The warrior wields his two swords.

hose

  • n. 输水软管；
  • a long tube made of rubber, plastic, etc., used for putting water onto fires, gardens, etc.
  • exp. Fireman pointed the hose at the fire, hoping to put it out.
  • v. 用水管冲洗 (汽车等)

onset

  • n. 开始；攻击，袭击
  • the beginning of something, especially something unpleasant
  • exp. The referee’s whistle indicates the onset of the game.

porcelain

  • n. 瓷，瓷器；
  • a hard white shiny substance made by baking clay and used for making delicate cups, plates and decorative objects; objects that are made of this
  • exp. I use a porcelain pot to serve my tea.
  • a. 精美的

utility

  • n. 功用，用途，公用事业，物业；
  • a service provided for the public, for example an electricity, water or gas supply; (formal) the quality of being useful; a piece of computer software that performs a particular task
  • exp. A Swiss Army knife isn’t a thing of beauty, but it does have a lot of utility.
  • a. 通用性的

nursery

  • n. 托儿所
  • a place where children are cared for during the day while their parents are working
  • exp. I drop off my kids at the nursery before I go to work.

infringe

  • v. 侵犯，违反，侵害
  • (of an action, a plan, etc.) to break a law or rule
  • exp. This magazine article infringed on the privacy of the actress.

perverse

  • a. 违反常情的；邪恶的
  • showing deliberate determination to behave in a way that most people think is wrong, unacceptable or unreasonable
  • exp. The fish is being deliberately perverse to swim to an opposite direction.

recede

  • vi. 退去；渐渐远去；(尤指问题、感情或品质) 主键减弱；(头发) 从前向后减少；向后倾斜
  • to move gradually away from somebody or away from a previous position
  • exp. As he got older his hairline began to recede.
