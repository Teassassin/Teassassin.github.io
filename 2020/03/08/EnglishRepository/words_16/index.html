百词斩 雅思 2020/03/08

total 31 words

set sth. back

  • v. 把 (钟、表) 向回拨
  • to slow the progress of
  • exp. The clock is fast. We need to set it back a few minutes.

excursion

  • n. 远足；短途旅行
  • a short journey made for pleasure, especially one that has been organized for a group of people
  • exp. We took an excursion into the forest.

zeal

  • n. 热情，热心
  • great energy or enthusiasm connected with something that you feel strongly about
  • exp. I can tell he is a soccer fanatic from the zeal he shows when watching the game.

tinge

  • v. 给···染色，轻微地影响；
  • a slight color, flavor, or quality
  • exp. The maple leaf was tinged red in autumn.

bolt

  • n. 门栓，螺栓；
  • a long, narrow piece of metal that you slide across the inside of a door or window in order to lock it
  • exp. The rusty metal bolt securely locked the door.
  • v. (马受惊) 脱缰

mackintosh

  • n. 雨衣，防水胶布
  • a waterproof raincoat made of rubberized fabric
  • exp. Dogs also need mackintochses to protect their fur from the rain.

turmoil

  • n. 骚动，骚乱
  • a state of great anxiety and confusion
  • exp. The naughty dog has caused absolute turmoil!

come to nothing

  • phr. 失败，落空
  • be unsuccessful
  • exp. All my shooting came to nothing; I never hit the target!

tract

  • n. 器官系统；一大片地
  • (biology) a system of connected organs or tissues along which materials or messages pass
  • exp. The digestive tract allows your body to use food for energy.

toddle

  • v. (如小孩) 蹒跚学步
  • to walk with short, unsteady steps
  • exp. The baby has just learnt to toddle, so he walks unsteadily.
  • n. 刚学走步的小孩

ponderous

  • a. 笨重的，笨拙的，沉闷的
  • slow or awkward because of weight and size
  • exp. The animal was gigantic and ponderous.

divert

  • v. 使转向，使改道 (或绕道)
  • to make somebody / something change the direction
  • exp. We’re being diverted from the road, but we’ll get back to it soon.

constituent

  • a. 组成的；有选举权的；
  • forming or helping to make a whole
  • exp. France is a constituent country of the EU.
    法国是欧盟的成员国
  • n. 选民

puncture

  • v. 刺破 (车胎等) ， 扎， 穿孔；
  • to make a small hole in something; to get a small hole; to suddenly make somebody feel less confident, proud, etc.
  • exp. One of the tyres was punctured by a nail.

peril

  • n. 危险
  • serious danger
  • exp. The man faced the peril of falling off the cliff.

junction

  • n. 路口；连接，接合
  • the place where two or more roads or railway / railroad lines meet
  • exp. Turn left at the junction of the two highways.

imminent

  • a. 迫近的，紧迫的，即将来临的
  • happening very soon
  • exp. The bomb’s detonation is imminent.

rise to the occasion

  • v. 应付自如
  • be equal to the occasion
  • exp. The game was hard, but the boy rose to the occasion and won the game.

antagonism

  • n. 敌对，对抗，敌意
  • a strong feeling of dislike or hatred : a desire to oppose something you dislike or disagree with
  • exp. There was a lot of antagonism between the competitors before the match.

perspire

  • v. 出汗，流汗
  • to produce a clear liquid from your skin when you are hot or nervous
  • exp. The two friends perspire from the heat.

make up with sb.

  • v. 与···和解
  • come to terms
  • exp. After the fight they made up with each other.

give (sth.) off

  • v. 发出 (烟，气)
  • send forth, or discharge; as of light, heat, or radiation, vapor, etc
  • exp. Guns give off smoke after they fire.

haven

  • n. 避难所
  • a place that is safe and peaceful where people or animals are protected
  • exp. The man finally escaped to an island, a safe haven from the scary sharks.

lax

  • a. 松懈的；【语】松弛元音的；
  • not strict, severe or careful enough about work, rules or standards of behaviour
  • exp. The security guard’s lax attitude towards his work endangers us all.
  • n. 松元音

quaint

  • a. 古雅的；精巧的
  • attractive in an unusual or old-fashioned way
  • exp. What a quaint town with its narrow streets and old-fashioned houses!

distill

  • v. 蒸馏
  • to make (a liquid) pure by heating it until it becomes a gas and then cooling it until it is a liquid again
  • exp. I learned how to distill water from the liquid in the lab.

dingy

  • a. 昏暗的，肮脏的
  • dark and dirty : not fresh or clean
  • exp. The room is old and dingy.

segregate

  • v. 分开，隔离，进行种族隔离
  • to separate people of different races, religious of sexes and treat them in a different way; to keep one thing separate from another
  • exp. The red man is segregated from the gold people.

elated

  • a. 振奋的；得意洋洋的
  • very happy and excited
  • exp. People were elated at the news that Beijing had won the bid of 2022 Winter Olympics.

take stock of

  • v. 估量，观察
  • to look at critically or searchingly, or in minute detail
  • exp. The businessman takes stock of the different options.
