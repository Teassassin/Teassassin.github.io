百词斩 雅思 2020/03/04

total 10 words

mitigate

  • v. 减轻，缓和
  • to make something less harmful, serious, etc.
  • exp. She takes the pill to mitigate the dizziness.

prevalent

  • a. 普遍的，流行的
  • that exists or is very common at a particular time or in a particular place
  • exp. It’s prevalent to use this kind of mobile telephones in the 90s.

predecessor

  • n. 前辈；(被取代的) 原有事物
  • a person who did a job before somebody else
  • exp. We should learn from and respect our predecessor’s experience.

come over sb.

  • v. (被感觉等) 支配，攥住，拜访
  • to suddenly feel something; to come and visit for a short time
  • exp. They were very happy when their granddaughter came over.

itinerary

  • n. 旅行计划
  • a plan of a journey, including the route and the places that you visit
  • exp. This is our itinerary of the trip to Europe.

pigment

  • n. 天然色素，颜料
  • a natural substance that gives color to animals and plants
  • exp. A pigment is a substance that gives something a particular color.

substantiate

  • v. 证实，使实体化
  • to prove the truth of (something)
  • exp. The shape of the prints substantiated that it was indeed a dog.

goad

  • vt. 刺激；鼓励
  • to urge or force (someone) to do something
  • exp. The boxer goads his opponent to disturb his mind.

hygiene

  • n. 卫生；卫生学；保健学
  • the practice of keeping yourself and your living and working areas clean in order to prevent illness and disease
  • exp. Hands should be washed with soap to improve hygiene.

renowned

  • a. 著名的
  • known and admired by many people for some special quality or achievement
  • exp. The Eiffel tower is renowned for its beauty and simplicity.
