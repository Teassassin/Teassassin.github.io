百词斩 雅思 2020/03/10

total 32 words 1 similar

pageant

  • n. 盛会，历史剧，选美
  • a play or performance made of scences from a historical event or a legend
  • exp. There is a pageant going on the stage.

cumbersome

  • a. 笨重的，麻烦的
  • hard to handle or manage because of size or weight
  • exp. This gift box is too cumbersome; I’ll never carry it home.

infirmary

  • n. 医院，医务室 (尤指学校等附设的病房或配药处)
  • a place where sick people stay and are cared for in a school, prison, summer camp, etc.
  • exp. An infirmary is a small hospital where patients are taken care of.

keep off

  • phr. 让开，不接近
  • refrain from entering or walking onto
  • exp. They sprayed the grass with poison, so you should keep off it.
  • refrain
    • v. 抑制，避免
    • n. 歌曲的反复句，叠句

volatile

  • a. 不稳定的；反复无常的
  • easily becoming a gas at a fairly low temperature
  • exp. This stock is very volatile, and it always experiences sharp increases and decreases.

pamphlet

  • n. 小册子
  • a very thin book with a paper cover, containing information about a particular subject
  • exp. The pamphlet will explain how our product works.

eddy

  • n. 旋涡；
  • a circular movement of air or water
  • exp. The little boat was almost caught in the eddy.
  • v. 旋转，起旋涡

ephemeral

  • a. 短暂的，短命的
  • lasting or used for only a short period of time
  • exp. The beauty of the flower is ephemeral; it lasts only a few minutes.

obsolete

  • a. 已过时的；已废弃的
  • no longer used because something new has been invented
  • exp. He put the obsolete pager into the drawer and picked up a mobile phone.
    他把过时的传呼机放进了抽屉，然后拿起了手机。
  • v. 使过时；使作废

pounce

  • n. 猛扑，飞扑；
  • vi. 猛扑，突然袭击；
  • to suddenly jump toward and take hold of someone or something
  • exp. “Ahhh!” I creamed as the cat pounced on to me.
  • vt. 扑过去抓住

beset

  • v. 镶嵌；困扰
  • to cause problems or difficulties for (someone or something)
  • exp. Various skin problems have beset her.

contrive

  • v. 发明；设计出；设法做到；谋划；策划
  • to think of or make something, for example a plan or a mathine, in a clever way; to manage to do something despite difficulties
  • exp. He contrived a clever method to cool his hot noodles.

throbbing

  • a. 跳动的，悸动的
  • pounding or beating strongly or violently
  • exp. His heart started throbbing as the beautiful girl walked by.

shimmer

  • v. 闪烁，微微发亮；
  • to shine with a light that seems to move slightly
  • exp. The sea was shimmering in the sunlight.
  • n. 微光，闪光

caustic

  • a. 腐蚀性的；
  • able to destroy or burn something by chemical action
  • exp. This sign is used as a warning for caustic chemicals.
  • n.焦散曲线

discern

  • v. 辨别；识别；看出
  • to know, recognize or understand something, especially something that is not obvious
  • exp. A night vision viewer helps to discern objects in the dark.

impede

  • v. 妨碍，阻碍
  • to delay or stop the progress of something
  • exp. The stones in the path impeded their hike.

revolve

  • v. (使) 旋转；(使) 绕转
  • to go in a circle around a central point
  • exp. This merry-go-round is revolving at a high speed.

decree

  • v. 命令，判决，颁布；
  • to decide, judge or order something officially
  • exp. The king decrees that everybody must give him money.
  • n. 法令，政令

muddle

  • n. 混乱，杂乱，(头脑的) 糊涂；
  • v. (使) 混乱， (使) 迷惑，混淆
  • exp. What a muddle these wires are in!

border on (upon)

  • v. 接近，近似
  • exp. The man was bordering on crying.

meagre

  • a. 瘦的，贫弱的，贫乏的
  • deficient in amount or quality or extent
  • exp. He leads a peagre life; on some days he doesn’t eat at all.

shoal

  • n. 浅滩，沙洲，一群 (鱼)；
  • a small, raised area of sand just below the surface of the water
  • exp. A shoal of fish are swimming together.
  • a. (水) 浅的；
  • v. (鱼) 成群集聚

superintend

  • v. 监督
  • to direct or manage (something, such as a project or activity)
  • exp. The young man’s job was to superintend the construction of the building.

lease

  • v. 出租，租得；
  • to use or let somebody use something, especially property or equipment, in exchange for rent or a regular payment
  • exp. He leased an expensive apartment for one year.
  • n. 租约，租契

intelligible

  • a. 可理解的，明白易懂的
  • that can be easily understood
  • exp. I think these simple math problems should be intelligible to you.

sediment

  • n. 沉渣，沉淀物，沉淀
  • the solid material that settles at the bottom of a liquid; sand, stones, mud, etc. carried by water or wind and left, for example, on the bottom of a lake, river, etc.
  • exp. The river has lots of muddy sediment underneath.

trickle

  • v. 一滴滴地流
  • to flow, or to make something flow, slowly in a thin stream
  • exp. The water trickles down from the tap.

meddle

  • vi. 干预，多管闲事
  • to become involved in the activities and concerns of other people when your involvement is not wanted
  • exp. His father is always meddling in his affairs, especially when he is playing computer games.
  • similar. muddle compare to this word above here

natal

  • a. 分娩的；与生俱来的
  • relating to or accompanying birth
  • exp. A water birth can help a mother to reduce natal pain.

chorus

  • n. 副歌部分；合唱团；合唱曲，合唱句；
  • part of a song that is sung after each verse
  • exp. The song’s chorus is my favorite part; we can all sing along.
  • v. 合唱，齐声说；齐声说

in sb.’s favour

  • phr. 对某人有利，偏爱
  • in one’s grant of favor or advantage to one over another (especially to a country or countries in matters of international trade, such as levying duties)
  • exp. I hate you - you never decide in my favour!
