百词斩 雅思 2020/03/12

total 29 words

overhaul

  • n. 大修；彻底革新；
  • an examination of a machine or system, including doing repairs on it or making changes to it
  • exp. This bathroom needs a major overhaul.
  • vt. 解体检修，大修

paranoia

  • n. (心理) 偏执狂，多疑症
  • exp. The man suffers from paranoia; he believes others are trying to harm him.

fatuous

  • a. 愚昧而不自知的
  • foolish or stupid
  • exp. The fatuous monarch had been manipulated by others.

equivalent

  • a. 相等的；等值的；
  • equal in value, amount, meaning, importance, etc.
  • exp. The price of the luxury car is equivalent to ten times his annual salary.
  • n. 同义词；等价物

scrupulous

  • a. 小心谨慎的，严谨的，细心的
  • very careful about doing something correctly
  • exp. The player looks scrupulous when he adjusts his position to hit the ball.

take sth. in

  • v. 改小尺寸；领会
  • know and comprehend the nature or meaning of
  • exp. The girl has to take in her dress a little to make it fit better.

excavate

  • v. 挖掘，挖出
  • to dig in the ground to look for old buildings or objects that have been buried for a long time; to find something by digging in this way
  • exp. This machine is excavating the ground.

haul

  • v. 拖，拉，拽；
  • to pull something / somebody with a lot of effort
  • exp. It is pointless for him to attempt to haul the truck barehanded.
  • n. 一次获得；
  • v. 硬拖，硬拉，用力拖

convene

  • v. 集合，召集，召唤
  • to arrange for people to come together for a formal meeting; to come together for a formal meeting
  • exp. We convened at the coffee shop.

pinpoint

  • v. 准确地解释或说明；
  • to find and show the exact position of somebody / something or the exact time that something happened; to be able to give the exact reason for something or to describe something exactly
  • exp. I pinpointed the enemy’s position with the flag.
  • a. 精确地；
  • n. 针尖，极小之物，极不重要的食物

retort

  • v. 反驳
  • to reply quickly to a comment, in an angry, offended or humorous way
  • exp. “You’re stupid!” I said; “So are you!” He retorted.
  • n. 反驳，反击，回嘴

pass sth. on to

  • v. 把···交给···
  • place into the hands or custody of
  • exp. She passed her old bracelet on to her daughter.

mercantile

  • a. 商人的，商业的
  • of or relating to the business of buying and selling products to earn money : of or relating to trade or merchants
  • exp. This is a mercantile container used for trading.

mannerism

  • n. (说话、举止等的) 习气，癖性，矫揉造作
  • a particular habit or way of speaking or behaving that somebody has but is not aware of
  • exp. One of the girl’s mannerisms is to bite her own toe.

tonic

  • n. 补药，主调音或基音；
  • a medicine that brings you back to a normal physical or mental condition
  • exp. Ginseng is a well-known tonic which is good for health.
  • a. 滋补的，声调的，使精神振作的

look in (on sb.)

  • v. (顺便) 看望
  • go to see in passing
  • exp. The boy wants to look in on his girlfriend, but her father is not very happy about it.

bring (call) sth. to mind

  • v. 想起某事
  • remind sb of doing sth
  • exp. Your words bring something to mind.

petition

  • n. 请愿书；祈求；
  • a written document signed by a large number of people that asks somebody in a position of authority to do or change something
  • exp. The villagers signed a petition asking for a new school.
  • v. 情愿

dodge

  • v. 躲避，避开；
  • to move quickly and suddenly to one side in order to avoid somebody / something
  • exp. Wow, look at him dodge all those bullets!
  • n. 躲闪，躲避；
  • v. 回避，规避

secretion

  • n. 分泌，分泌物，隐藏
  • the production of a liquid by part of a plant or animal
  • exp. The snail produces a green secretion as it moves.

laudable

  • a. 值得称赞的
  • deserving praise
  • exp. Her performance was laudable: everybody loved it.

pay sb. back

  • v. 受到惩罚
  • do or give something to somebody in return
  • exp. The forest animals will pay the hunter back for what he’s done.

intersection

  • n. 交点，十字路口
  • a place where two or more roads, lines, etc. meet or cross each other; the act of intersecting something
  • exp. Meet me at the intersection of two streets.

ascribe

  • v. 把···归因于；把···归属于
  • to consider that something is caused by a particular thing or person
  • exp. We can ascribe his illness to undue work pressure.

ruinous

  • a. 毁坏的，毁灭性的，破坏性的
  • causing or likely to cause damage or destruction
  • exp. The ruinous tornado completely destroyed our town.

platitude

  • n. 陈词滥调，老生常谈
  • a comment or statement that has been made very often before and is therefore not interesting
  • exp. “No pain, no gain” is not a platitude, but a truth.

surgeon

  • n. 外科医生
  • a doctor who is trained to perform surgery (= medical operations that involve cutting open a person’s body)
  • exp. My surgeon decided to give me an operation.

vomit

  • v. 呕吐；涌出；
  • to have the food, liquid, etc., that is in your stomach come out through your mouth because you are sick
  • exp. He isn’t really vomiting; it’s just an eye trick.
  • n. 呕吐物，呕吐

malignant

  • a. 恶性的，恶毒的，充满恨意的
  • (of a tumor or disease) that cannot be controlled and is likely to cause death; (formal) having or showing a strong desire to harm somebody
  • exp. The tumor is malignant - we will have to remove it.
