百词斩 雅思 2020/03/21

total 19 words

session

  • n. (法庭的) 开庭；(议会等的) 开会
  • a formal meeting or series of meetings of a court, a parliament, etc.; a period of time when such meetings are held; a period of time that is spent doing a particular activity
  • exp. The court is in session, and the lawyer is presenting the evidence.

maneuver

  • v. 操纵；
  • a movement performed with care and skill; a clever plan, action or movement that is used to give somebody an advantage
  • exp. The skilled pilot can maneuver the helicopter very well.
  • n. 花招

hazardous

  • a. 有危险的；碰运气的
  • involving risk or danger, especially to somebody’s health or safety
  • exp. It is hazardous to touch the tiger’s butt.

hurl

  • v. 用力投掷
  • to throw something / somebody violently in a particular direction
  • exp. When she graduated, she hurled her books into the air.

portfolio

  • n. 公文包，文件夹，投资组合
  • a thin flat case used for carrying documents, drawings, etc.; (finance) a set of shares owned by a particular person or organization
  • exp. He checked his portfolio to find some documents.

practicable

  • a. 能实行的，行得通的
  • able to be done; likely to be successful
  • exp. The boy put forward a practicable solution to get the weight of the elephant.

intent

  • a. 全神贯注的；专心致志的；
  • exp. He looked at the computer screen with an intent expression.
  • n. 意图；意向；目的

gloomy

  • a. 愁容满面的，阴暗的，没有希望的
  • nearly dark, or badly lit in a way that makes you feel sad; sad and without hope; without much hope of success or happiness in the future
  • exp. The little boy is in a really gloomy mood.

appreciable

  • a. 可察觉的；很可观的；可感知的；可评估的
  • large enough to be noticed or thought important
  • exp. The girl went through an appreciable change in looks.

excess

  • a. 额外的，过量的，过度的，附加的；
  • in addition to an amount that is necessary, usual or legal
  • exp. A man threw an excess of chickpea into the bin after dinner.
  • n. 超越，超过，过度

nevertheless

  • adv. 然而，不过；尽管如此，不过，仍然
  • despite something that you have just mentioned
  • exp. The dog laughed at the rabbit. Nevertheless, the rabbit still helped him out.

fret

  • v. 使烦恼，使焦急；
  • to be worried or unhappy and not able to relax
  • exp. Don’t fret; I’m sure your dog will be fine!
  • n. (斑卓琴、吉他、曼陀林琴等弦乐器指板上定音的) 档

rectify

  • v. 纠正；修复
  • to put right something that is wrong
  • exp. I made a mistake, so I quickly rectified it.

terrific

  • a. 极好的；可怕的；(尺寸或程度) 极其巨大的，大的惊人的
  • excellent; wonderful
  • exp. She feels terrific as she has successfully lost weight.

preceding

  • a. 在前的，在先的
  • to happen before something or come before something / somebody in order; to go in front of somebody
  • exp. Click this button to browse the preceding text.

cart

  • n. 手推车，运货马车；
  • exp. Carts are useful for carrying goods in the supermarket.
  • v. 用车装载

defer

  • v. 使推迟，使延期；
  • to delay something until a later time
  • exp. This country deferred the male citizens’ retirement age from 60 to 70.

wrench

  • n. 扳手；痛苦，难受；
  • a metal tool with a specially shaped end for holding and turning things, including one which can be adjusted to fit objects of different sizes, also called a monkey wrench or an adjustable spanner
  • exp. The worker used a wrench to repair the sink.
  • v. 使扭伤

comic

  • a. 滑稽的，喜剧的，漫画的；
  • amusing and making you laugh; connected with comedy (= entertainment that is funny and that makes people laugh)
  • exp. His comic actions often make us laugh.
  • n. 连环漫画杂志
