百词斩 雅思 2020/03/18

total 17 words 1 opposite

precipitate

  • a. 突如其来的，仓促的，迅猛的；
  • to suddenly force somebody / something into a particular state or condition
  • exp. They did not expect the rain to be precipitate as quickly as it did.
  • v. 【气象】凝结；促成，使···沉淀，猛摔；
  • n. 沉淀物，冷凝物

detach

  • v. 使分开，拆卸
  • to remove something from something larger; to become separated from something; to leave or separate yourself from somebody / something
  • exp. I detach the screen protector from my phone.
  • opposite. attach

lapse

  • n. 失误，过失；(时间) 流逝；
  • a small mistake, especially one that is caused by forgetting something or by being careless
  • exp. I had a lapse in judgment and put too much soap in the laundry.
  • v. 背弃，放弃 (宗教信仰)

ascertain

  • v. 探知，弄清，查明 (真相等)
  • to find out the true or correct information about something
  • exp. The detective searched this room to ascertain the facts of the case.

disclose

  • v. 公开，揭露，泄露
  • to make (something) known to the public
  • exp. He disclosed the classified secret to the public

pastime

  • n. 消遣，娱乐
  • something that you enjoy doing when you are not working
  • exp. Reading is my favorite pastime.

hectic

  • a. 紧张的，忙碌的，患肺病的，(因患肺病等) 发烧的，有病态潮红的脸上发红，发热的，兴奋的，狂热的，肺病的
  • very busy and filled with activity
  • exp. She hopes to have more hands to end this hectic day!

lofty

  • a. 极高的，巍峨的
  • (of buildings, mountains, etc.) very high and impressive
  • exp. This mountain’s lofty peak towers through the clouds.

strenuous

  • a. 费力的
  • needing great effort and energy
  • exp. It’s a strenuous thing for him to raise the weights.

conceal

  • v. 隐藏，隐瞒，掩盖
  • to hide somebody / something
  • exp. I use this mask to conceal my face.

foil

  • n. 锡箔纸；衬托；
  • metal made into very thin sheets that is used for covering or wrapping things, especially food
  • exp. The dark chocolate cakes in foil cups are put on the table.
  • v. 挫败；使受挫折

hoist

  • v. 升起，吊起；向上推；
  • to raise or pull something up to a higher position, often using ropes or special equipment
  • exp. We use the machine to hoist the huge stone off the ground.
  • n. 起重机

shrewd

  • a. 精明的；机灵的
  • clever at understanding and making judgements about a situation
  • exp. He was a shrewd businessman who was careful with every penny.

consecutive

  • a. 连续的，连贯的
  • following one after another in a series, without interruption
  • exp. She has been the top student in her class for three consecutive years.

deceive

  • v. 欺骗，行骗
  • to make (someone) believe something that is not true
  • exp. The man deceives others by wearing a hilarious mask.

elite

  • n. 精英
  • a small group of people in a society, etc. who are powerful and have a lot of influence, because they are rich, intelligent, etc.
  • exp. Only a few elites can make it to the top of the pyramid.

circumference

  • n. 圆周长度，圆周；周围
  • the length of a line that goes around something or that makes a circle or other round shape; the external boundary or surface of a figure or object
  • exp. Can you measure the circumference of this apple?
