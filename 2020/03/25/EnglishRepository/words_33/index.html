百词斩 雅思 2020/03/25

total 13 words 1 similar

preliminary

  • a. 初步的，预备的；
  • happening before a more important action or event
  • exp. The preliminary drawings of the building are already finished.
  • n. 预赛；预考，初始；初步措施

composite

  • a. 合成的，复合的；
  • made of different parts or materials
  • exp. She made a composite photo of herself at the beach.
  • n. 合成物，复合材料

arrogant

  • a. 傲慢的，妄自尊大的
  • behaving in a proud, unpleasant way, showing little thought for other people
  • exp. The arrogant man refused to listen to anyone else’s ideas.

stimulus

  • n. 鼓励；促进因素
  • something that helps somebody / something to develop better or more quickly
  • exp. A big red flower means a stimulus to her.

surge

  • v. 波浪般汹涌奔腾；激增；
  • to move very quickly and suddenly in a particular direction
  • exp. The water surges over the road.
  • n. 波涛汹涌
  • similar. surgeon
    • n. 外科医生

discrete

  • a. 分开的；不连续的
  • independent of other things of the same type
  • exp. The abacus is made up of discrete units.
    算盘是由分开的单元组成的。

elastic

  • a. 可伸缩的，(系统或计划) 灵活的；
  • that can change or be changed
  • exp. The spring is very elastic thing that can stretch far.
  • n. 松紧带，橡皮圈

obscene

  • a. 下流的，淫秽的
  • exp. The male monkey makes obscene display of his red buttock to attract the female.

illiterate

  • n. 文盲，目不识丁者；
  • (of a person) not knowing how to read or write
  • exp. Illiterates cannot read.
  • a. 缺乏 (某方面) 知识的

set off

  • phr. 出发
  • to begin a journey
  • exp. The pirates set of from the island.

loom

  • n. 织布机；
  • a machine for making cloth by twisting threads betwween other threads which go in a different direction
  • exp. The girl is operating the loom to weave the cloth.
  • v. 阴森地逼近；隐现；即将来临

stitch

  • v. 缝，绣；
  • to use a needle and thread to repair, join, or decorate pieces of cloth; to sew the edges of a wound together
  • exp. The cobbler is stitching a part of the shoe using the sewing machine.
    鞋匠用缝纫机来缝合鞋的部件。

restrain

  • v. 抑制 (愤怒等)，控制
  • to stop somebody / something from doing something, especially by using physical force; to stop yourself from feeling an emotion or doing something that you would like to do
  • exp. I must restrain my anger because he is the boss.
