百词斩 雅思 2020/03/27

total 13 words

dilapidated

  • a. 破旧的，破烂的
  • in very bad condition because of age or lack of care
  • exp. This house is dilapidated! I couldn’t possibly live here.

dart

  • n. 飞镖；猛冲；标枪；
  • a small pointed object, sometimes with feathers to help it fly, that is shot as a weapon or thrown in the game of darts
  • exp. We love to throw darts for fun.
  • v. 快速投掷；突然移动

bud

  • n. 蓓蕾，芽
  • a small lump that grows on a plant and from which a flower, leaf or stem develops; a flower or leaf that is not fully open; used to speak to a maan you do not know
  • exp. The flower has not bloomed yet, but I can see the bud.

apparatus

  • n. 装置，仪器；设备
  • a tool or piece of equipment used for specific activities
  • exp. The apparatus here can be used to make a great cup of coffee.

encyclopedia

  • n. 百科全书
  • a book or set of books giving information about all areas of knowledge or about different areas of one particular subject, usually arranged in alphabetical order; a similar collection of information on a website or CD-ROM
  • exp. This plant encyclopedia has many beautiful illustrations.

confer

  • v. 授予 (奖项、学位、荣誉或权利) ；商讨，协商
  • to give somebody an award, a university degree or a particular honor or right
  • exp. The president conferred a degree onto the graduating student.

banquet

  • n. (正式的) 宴会；盛宴
  • a formal meal for a large number of people, usually for a special occasion, at which speeches are often made
  • exp. The banquet had many different kinds of food.

lure

  • v. 以诱饵吸引；
  • to persuade or trick somebody to go somewhere or to do something by promising them a reward
  • exp. I lure fish by putting worms on my fishing pole.
  • n. 诱惑物

rig

  • n. 钻井架；装备；帆具；成套器械；
  • (especially in compounds) a large piece of equipment that is used for taking oil or gas from the ground or the bottom of the sea
  • exp. The oil rig extracts oil from the ocean.
  • v. 操纵；装配；欺骗

momentum

  • n. (物理) 动量；势头；动力
  • a force that is gained by movement
  • exp. His momentum caused him to go up into the air.

indispensable

  • a. 必不可少的；必需的
  • too important to be without
  • exp. WiFi is indispensable in modern life.

sanction

  • n. 批准；
  • an official order that limits trade, contact, etc. with a particular country, in order to make it do something, such as obeying international law
  • exp. The installation of air conditioners received sanction from the boss.
  • v. 批准，支持，对···实施制裁

moist

  • a. (表面) 潮湿的，湿润的
  • slightly wet
  • exp. The grass is moist due to last night’s heavy rain.
