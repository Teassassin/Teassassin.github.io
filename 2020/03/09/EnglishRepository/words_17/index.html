百词斩 雅思 2020/03/09

total 22 words

pilgrim

  • n. 朝圣者；香客
  • a person who travels to a holy place for religious reasons
  • exp. The pilgrims pray sincerely while approaching the Potala Palace.

misrepresent

  • vi. 作虚假陈述；
  • to describe (someone or something) in a false way especially in order to deceive someone : to give someone a false idea about (something or someone)
  • exp. They misrepresent her character - she’s not really that bad.
  • vt. 歪曲，误传

blot

  • n. 墨水渍；
  • a spot or stain
  • exp. Water color paintings consist of many blots of paint.
  • v. (用吸水纸) 吸干墨水

ravage

  • v. 摧残，破坏
  • to damage or harm (something) very badly
  • exp. The fire ravaged the city.
  • n. 毁坏，荒废，破坏的残迹

malice

  • n. 恶意，怨恨
  • a feeling of hatred for somebody that causes a desire to harm them
  • exp. He punctured the bicycle tyres out of pure malice.

bewilder

  • v. 使不知所措；使迷惑
  • to confuse (someone) very much
  • exp. She is bewildered; she does not know what to do.

ransom

  • n. 赎金，赎身；
  • to pay money in order to free (a person who has been captured or kidnapped)
  • exp. The evil man kidnapped a boy and held him for ransom.
  • v. 赎回，解救

audit

  • v. 审计，查账；旁听；
  • to officially examine the financial accounts of a company
  • exp. My main job is to audit the account every day.
  • n. 审计；查账

denomination

  • n. (货币的) 面额；教派
  • the denomination of a banknote or coin is its official value
  • exp. These banknotes com in various denominations.

bring about

  • phr. 导致，引起
  • cause to happen, occur or exist
  • exp. Such a small issue brought about a quarrel between the kids.

be on good terms (with sb.)

  • phr. 关系好
  • be close friends
  • exp. Though they are competitors, they are still on good terms with each other.

bulk

  • n. 大批；容量；体积；大块；
  • the (large) size or quantity of something
  • exp. Buying tomatoes in bulk is more economical than buying in small quantities.
  • a. 大量的；散装的；
  • v. 膨胀；显得重要

come upon

  • phr. 遇到
  • to meet or encounter unexpectedly
  • exp. The couple came upon each other while walking their dogs.

warehouse

  • n. 仓库
  • a building where large quantities of goods are stored, especially before they are sent to shops / stores to be sold
  • exp. The warehouse is full of boxes.

quiver

  • vi. 颤抖；
  • to shake slightly; to make a slight movement
  • exp. This boy was quivering as he walked down the street on a cold day.
  • n. 颤抖；箭袋

chaste

  • a. 朴素的；贞洁的
  • not having sex
  • exp. The monk lives a chaste and simple life.

discord

  • n. 不和，争吵；(声音) 不和谐
  • lack of agreement between people, ideas, etc.
  • exp. Fights can result in lasting discord between people.

disdain

  • n. 蔑视，鄙视；
  • a feeling of strong dislike or disapproval of someone or something you think does not deserve respect
  • exp. Her facial expression shows her disdain for the girl beside her.
  • vt. 鄙视

set on sb.

  • v. 攻击
  • attack someone physically or emotionally
  • exp. The runaway criminal was set on by a police dog.

mandatory

  • a. 命令的，强制性的
  • required by a law or rule
  • exp. Uniform hairstyle is a mandatory requirement for military training.

dreary

  • a. 乏味的；沉闷的
  • causing unhappiness or sad feelings : not warm, cheerful, etc.
  • exp. It is very dreary to do the same job every day.

pesticide

  • n. 杀虫剂
  • a chemical used for killing pests, especially insects
  • exp. Farmers will spray pesticides to prevent crops from being eaten by insects.
