百词斩 雅思 2020/03/19

total 22 words 1 opposite

stagnant

  • a. 不流动的
  • water or air is not moving and therefore smells unpleasant
  • exp. The pond looks very stagnant and probably doesn’t have fish.

clockwise

  • a. 顺时针方向的；
  • moving around in the same direction as the hands of a clock
  • exp. The man is walking around the garden in a clockwise direction.
  • adv. 顺时针方向地

elaborate

  • a. 精心设计的，精巧的；
  • very complicated and detailed; carefully prepared and orgnized
  • exp. The design of this church is very elaborate.
  • v. 详细说明，详尽阐述

subsequent

  • a. 随后的
  • happening or coming after something else
  • exp. Press this button to play the subsequent track.

concede

  • v. 承认失败；承认；让步
  • to admit that something is true, logical, etc.
  • exp. In the fight with this crazy kangaroo, I had no choice but to concede.

flank

  • n. 肋，肋腹，侧翼；
  • the side of an animal between the ribs and the hip; the side of something such as a building or mountain; the side of something such as a building or mountain; the left or right side of an army during a battle, or a sports team during a game
  • exp. Riders will dig their spurs into horses’ flanks in order to make them run faster.
  • v. 位于···的侧面

consolidate

  • v. 把···合为一体
  • to join things together into one; to be joined into one
  • exp. The two private taxi companies have been consolidated into one.

malnutrition

  • n. 营养不良
  • a poor condition of health caused by a lack of food or a lack of the right type of food
  • exp. The women suffering from malnutrition is very weak.

nuisance

  • n. 讨厌的人；麻烦事；伤害
  • a thing, person or situation that is annoying or cause trouble or problems
  • exp. The boy who keeps pulling the girl’s hair is really a nuisance!

spontaneous

  • a. 自发的，无意识的；自然的，天真率直的
  • not planned but done because you suddenly want to do it; often doing things without planning to, because you suddenly want to do them
  • exp. The father made a spontaneous decision to take his family to the beach that day.

baffle

  • v. 难住；使困惑；阻碍；
  • to confuse somebody completely; to be too difficult or strange for somebody to understand or explain
  • exp. The math problem really baffled him.
  • n. 挡住

intricate

  • a. 错综复杂的
  • having a lot of different parts and small details that fit together
  • exp. The inside of this watch is quite intricate.

bleak

  • a. 凄凉的；萧瑟的；阴郁的；无希望的
  • (of a place) exposed, empty, or with no pleasant features
  • exp. This place is so bleak and depressing.

eligible

  • a. 适合被选的，符合条件的；(尤指婚姻等) 合适的，合意的
  • a person who is eligible for something or to do something, is able to have or do it because they have the right qualifications, are the right age, etc.
  • exp. In China, you are eligible to get your driver license as early as 18.

partial

  • a. 不完全的；偏爱的，特别喜欢的；偏袒的，不公平的，有偏见的；部分的
  • showing or feeling too much support for one person, tam, idea, etc., in away that is unfair
  • exp. The partial eclipse obscured less than half the moon’s surface.
  • opposite. impartial
  • a. 公平的，不偏不倚的

segment

  • n. 部分；片段；(橘子等的) 瓣
  • a part of something that is separate from the other parts or can be considered separately
  • exp. Our company dominates a small segment of the market.

afflict

  • v. 使痛苦；使苦恼
  • to affect somebody / something in an unpleasant or harmful way
  • exp. She is afflicted by a bad headache.

adverse

  • a. 不利的；(方向上) 逆的；在相反的方向上移动 (或运转)； 相反的
  • negative and unpleasant; not likely to produce a good result
  • exp. Bad teeth are one of the adverse effects of drinking too many carbonated beverages.

outlet

  • n. 出口；表现机会，发泄方法
  • a pipe or hole through which liquid or gas can flow out
  • exp. Water came out of the wall through several outlets.

surpass

  • v. 胜出；超出
  • to do or be better than somebody / something
  • exp. The turtle surpassed the rabbit in the running race.

cater

  • v. 供应伙食；为 (集会、婚礼等) 承办酒席
  • to provide food and drinks for a social event
  • exp. We are able to cater for weddings, parties and banquets.
